Steve Jobs (1955-2011)

Introduction

Steve Jobs was an American entrepreneur and inventor who co-founded Apple Inc. and helped revolutionize the personal computer industry with the Macintosh. He was known for his innovative design philosophy and his ability to create products that were both user-friendly and aesthetically pleasing.

Early Life

Steve Jobs was born on February 24, 1955 in San Francisco, California. His biological parents, Joanne Schieble and Abdulfattah Jandali, were unmarried graduate students who put him up for adoption. He was adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs, who raised him in Mountain View, California. Jobs showed an early interest in electronics and gadgetry, and often worked on projects with his father in their garage.

Apple Inc.

In 1976, Steve Jobs co-founded Apple Computer Inc. with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. Their first product, the Apple I, was a personal computer kit that was hand-built by Wozniak and sold by Jobs. In 1984, Apple introduced the Macintosh, which was the first commercially successful personal computer to feature a graphical user interface.

Later Life and Legacy

Steve Jobs resigned as CEO of Apple in 2011 due to health issues. He passed away on October 5, 2011 at the age of 56. Jobs is remembered for his contributions to the personal computer industry and his innovative design philosophy. He was also known for his memorable speeches and presentations, including his commencement address at Stanford University in 2005.
